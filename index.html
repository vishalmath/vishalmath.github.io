Brief Biography

Hi! I am a first-year graduate student at Louisiana State University. My mentor is Gene Kopp. My main research interest is analytic number theory, though I'm generally a fan of number theory of all flavors. You can find my CV here.

Previously, I received my M.Sc. from the NIT Manipur. I am truly blessed to have Shanta Laishram as a master's thesis advisor at the Indian Statistical Institute Delhi. I completed my Bachelors's from GD Goenka University Gurugram under the guidance of Smita Sood. In the Summer of 2018, I was a research intern at IIT Guwahati, working with Natesan Srinivasan.

Theses

  • Prime Divisors in an Arithmetic Progression and Explicit ABC Conjecture [pdf]
    Vishal Bhardwaj
    MSc Thesis (NIT Manipur), 2022
    Abstract. We discussed a brief survey on refinements and generalizations of Sylvester's theorem, which states that a product of $k$ consecutive integers, each of which exceeds $k$, is divisible by a prime greater than $k$. These include the statements of some results. We see Baker's explicit abc-conjecture. The proof of explicit abc - conjecture results are followed to find the values of $\epsilon$, $\omega_{\epsilon}$, and $N_{\epsilon}$.
